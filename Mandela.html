Nelson Rolihlala Mandela

July 18, 1918 - December 5, 2013

Nelson Mandela was a global symbol of resistance against apartheid in South Africa. A key figure in the anti-apartheid movement, Mandela's commitment to justice led to his imprisonment for 27 years. Upon his release in 1990, he played a pivotal role in ending apartheid and became South Africa's first black president in 1994. Mandela's presidency focused on reconciliation and building a united nation. His enduring legacy lies in his emphasis on forgiveness and inclusivity, leaving the world with a powerful message: "It always seems impossible until it's done." Mandela died but his legacy lives on as an inspiration for resilience and courage.
